Privacy Policy

Effective Date: October 2025
Last Updated: October 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to WhyTF Health (“we,” “our,” “us”). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal data when you visit whytfhealth.com or interact with our content, contact forms, or analytics tools.

We are based in the European Union and comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”) and applicable data protection laws. Our guiding principle is simple: we collect the least data necessary, store it securely, and never sell or misuse it.

2. Data We Collect

We collect only the information required to operate this website responsibly.

2.1 Information You Provide Voluntarily

  • Contact Form: When you submit a message through our contact form, we receive your name, email address, and message content. This information is used solely to respond to your inquiry.
  • Email correspondence: If you contact us directly, we will store your message and contact details to follow up.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Analytics: We use a privacy-friendly analytics provider (Umami) that does not use cookies or personal identifiers. It collects anonymous data such as page views, browser type, country, and referring site.
  • Server logs: Our hosting provider automatically logs basic information (IP address, browser, date/time of access) to maintain website security. These logs are automatically purged after a short retention period.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To respond to messages and feedback submitted via the contact form.
  • To improve site content and technical performance using aggregated, non-personal analytics.
  • To protect the security and integrity of our website.
  • To comply with legal obligations or respond to lawful requests from public authorities, if required.

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or automated decision-making.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6)

We process your data under one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Consent: When you voluntarily send information through the contact form.
  • Legitimate interests: To maintain and secure the website and analyse aggregated traffic patterns.
  • Legal obligation: If required to disclose information to comply with EU or Member State law.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected.

  • Contact form submissions are deleted within 12 months of resolution, unless a longer retention is legally required.
  • Server logs are retained for a maximum of 30 days.
  • Anonymous analytics data are stored in aggregated form indefinitely and cannot identify you.

6. Data Sharing & Transfers

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with advertisers or unrelated third parties. The limited sharing that occurs is strictly for operational purposes:

  • Hosting: Our website is hosted by a trusted EU or EEA-based provider bound by GDPR-compliant data-processing terms.
  • Analytics: Umami operates servers within the EU and stores only aggregated, non-personal data.
  • Email delivery: Contact-form submissions are securely processed via Formspree, which adheres to GDPR requirements.

No data are transferred outside the EEA unless adequate safeguards (e.g. Standard Contractual Clauses) are in place.

7. Your Rights Under the GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access – request a copy of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification – correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure – request deletion of your data (“right to be forgotten”).
  • Restriction – limit how your data are processed.
  • Portability – obtain your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection – object to data processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent – where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

To exercise these rights, contact us at: contact@whytfhealth.com. We will respond within 30 days as required by GDPR.

8. Security of Your Data

We apply industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect data against loss, misuse, or unauthorised access. While no online platform is completely risk-free, we continuously review security practices, use HTTPS encryption, and limit access to personal data to authorised individuals only.

9. Cookies & Tracking

We do not use cookies for advertising or behavioural tracking. Essential cookies (if any) are strictly necessary for website operation and do not require consent under GDPR Article 6(1)(f). We use Umami Analytics, which runs cookie-free and does not store personal identifiers.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our content is educational and suitable for general audiences but not specifically directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us to remove it.

11. External Links

Our articles and pages may contain links to third-party websites (e.g., journals, public health organisations). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those external sites and encourage you to review their policies.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect new regulations or operational changes. The “Last Updated” date at the top will always show the latest version.

13. Contact for Data Protection Queries

For questions or requests related to your personal data or this policy, contact us at: privacy@whytfhealth.com

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority (DPA) within the EU if you believe your data rights have been violated.
