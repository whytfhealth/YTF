Adverse Event — What It Really Means

Reporting everything after a shot keeps the safety net tight.

Estimated read time: ~1.5 min

Adverse event doesn’t mean “caused by the vaccine.” It means “happened after, so we log it.” Sore arm, headache, low fever — common and self-limited. Serious events, like anaphylaxis, are extremely rare, treated immediately, and analyzed to see if there’s a real link. Open reporting (VAERS, Yellow Card, EudraVigilance) is a smoke alarm: sensitive by design so investigators can sort signal from coincidence.

Why It Matters

Transparent reporting builds trust and catches true safety issues early, while reminding us that most reactions are expected and mild.

Closing Line

“Adverse event” is a log entry, not an indictment.
